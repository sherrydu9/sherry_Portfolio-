VISIT THE SITE

OVERVIEW

Wudog is a fully responsive e-commerce website that aims to sell the dog's clothes, food and toys. Written in HTML, CSS, Javascript, PHP, and MySQL. It is a fully functional and dynamic prototype of a store website.

BACKGROUND

Sherry Du is a designer, creative thinker and maker. She wants to sell dog stuffs for dog lovers. She drew a lot of illustrations and took pictures of puppy stuffs for the website. This website is dedicated to selling and promoting Sherry’s work through a fun and engaging way.

The goal of this website had to meet the following objectives:

  • A store site prototype
  • Fully responsive design
  • Database driven by MySQL
  • Functional features i.e. sorting, price calculations
  • All images are original content

PROBLEMS

1. Buying dog items is not easy for people with disabilities or the elderly.

2. Dog owners want to spend as much time as possible with their dogs, but shopping is very time consuming.

3. There is no centralized location for dog treats and toys so dog owners have to shop in multiple locations, which is inconvenient.

SOLUTIONS

E-commerce helps pet owners through giving them choice, particularly for those who need specialist diets that are difficult to source.

Pet owners also need to buy a high volume of product compared to other categories, so e-commerce allows them to harness direct delivery and this facilitates sourcing products through the specialist pet trade rather than through normal supermarket shopping trips.

FINAL DESIGN

Powerful filters

On the product list page, users are able to filter through all the products simply by its type, price and names.

Quick Checkout

The checkout page is a fully dynamic and users can be able to edit, delete and change quantity of the products in cart. They can also get a real-time estimated calculation of the total price plus tax and shipping.

CONCLUSION

The biggest challenge for me was definitely the backend aspect - utilizing Ajax, PHP, and MySQL to create a database. It was the first time that I've ever done something like this so I had to spend a lot of time troubleshooting. Through the process I learned about backend server development and how to more efficiently organize my code.
